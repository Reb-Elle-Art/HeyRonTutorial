Your Workspace (Where Files Live)

Your agent works in the same workspace you see in your browser — but there's a layer between you.

The Simple Version

You see a file browser in your browser window. Your agent sees the same files, but works inside a protected space called a container.

Think of it like this: You're looking through a window at a workshop. Your agent is inside the workshop, building things. You can see what they're making, and you can pass notes back and forth, but you're not physically inside.

What You Start With

When you first log in, your workspace contains six files:

  • AGENTS.md — How your agent operates, protocols, procedures
  • HEARTBEAT.md — Periodic tasks your agent checks
  • IDENTITY.md — Quick reference for your agent's name/persona
  • SOUL.md — Your agent's core philosophy and voice
  • TOOLS.md — Available tools, API keys, setup notes
  • USER.md — Information about you (the human)

These files are how you and your agent stay coordinated. Edit them to change how your agent behaves.

The Path

Both you and your agent use the same path:

/root/.openclaw/workspace/

This is where all files live. When your agent creates a file, it appears here. When you upload a file through the interface, your agent can see it.

The "Nerve"

The connection between your browser and your agent's container is called the nerve. It's the pathway for:

  • Your messages to reach your agent
  • Your agent's files to appear in your browser
  • Updates to flow back and forth

You don't need to manage the nerve. It just works. But it's useful to know it exists — when files don't appear immediately, the nerve is usually syncing.

What This Means Practically

Creating Files

Ask your agent: "Create a file called notes.md" — it appears in your workspace.

Reading Files

Your agent can read any file you see. Just ask: "What's in the AGENTS.md file?"

Getting Files Out

Here's the important part: Files in the workspace are not directly accessible outside the container.

To get a file to your local computer, you need a tool:

  • GitHub — Push files to a repository, then download
  • AgentMail — Email files to yourself
  • Cloud storage — Sync to Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.

That's what the Toolkit section covers.

Key Takeaway

The workspace is shared, but isolated. Your agent can create, read, and organize files. You can see them in your browser. But to get them onto your actual computer, you need to set up a connection (GitHub, email, etc.).

What to Do Now

  1. Look at your file browser in the Control interface
  2. Confirm you see the six default files
  3. Ask your agent: "What files are in our workspace?"
  4. Have your agent create a test file
  5. Watch it appear in your browser
