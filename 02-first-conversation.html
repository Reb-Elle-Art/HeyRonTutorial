First Conversation_

Your first session sets the tone for the entire partnership. A few intentional choices now will save confusion later.

Frame the Relationship

Start with a short statement about how you want to work together:

"I want to work with you as a partner, not a tool. I prefer concise replies, but be thorough when it matters. I'll correct you when needed — don't take it personally."

Establish Preferences

  • Response style: Do you want short replies or detailed ones?
  • Initiative level: Should the agent propose things or only act when asked?
  • Correction style: Direct correction or explanation of what went wrong?

Give a Concrete First Task

Don't just test — actually work on something. Fetch a file, summarize a document, create a checklist. The agent learns by doing, not by probing.

What to Avoid

  • Vague open-ended questions ("What can you do?")
  • Long orientation sessions — learn by doing
  • Assuming the agent knows your preferences without telling it
