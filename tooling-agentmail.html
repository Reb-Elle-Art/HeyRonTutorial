AgentMail — Email for Your Agent

Give your agent its own email inbox to send, receive, and manage emails.

At a Glance

What you'll have: An email address like your-agent@agentmail.to that your agent can use to send and receive emails.

Why it matters: Your agent can email you updates, receive documents, communicate with services, and act as an independent identity.

Try It First

Before setting up, explore what AgentMail does:

  1. Go to console.agentmail.to
  2. Click around the console (you can browse before signing up)
  3. Ask yourself: "What would my agent email me about?"

Then come back. The exploration helps it stick.

What This Is (Simple Version)

AgentMail gives your AI agent its own email inbox — just like Gmail, but built for agents.

  • Send emails: Your agent can email you, others, or services
  • Receive emails: People can email your agent directly
  • API-first: No complex setup, just an API key
  • Usage-based: Pay only for what you use, not per inbox

Think of it as: A mailbox for your agent — it can send letters, receive replies, and manage correspondence on your behalf.

What You Do vs. What Your Agent Does

👤 YOU Do This (One-time setup):

  1. Create an AgentMail account
  2. Generate an API key
  3. Give the API key to your agent

🤖 YOUR AGENT Does This (Ongoing):

  1. Create and manage email inboxes
  2. Send emails on your behalf
  3. Check for and read incoming emails
  4. Handle attachments and replies

Prerequisites

Before You Start

  • An email address (for your AgentMail account)
  • 5 minutes to complete setup

Step-by-Step Setup

Step 1: Create Your AgentMail Account

  1. Go to console.agentmail.to
  2. Click "Sign Up"
  3. Enter your email and create a password
  4. Verify your email (check your inbox for a confirmation link)

Ask your agent: "Help me set up AgentMail" if you get stuck.

Step 2: Generate an API Key

  1. Log into the AgentMail Console
  2. Look for "API Keys" in the sidebar or dashboard
  3. Click "Create New API Key"
  4. Give it a name like "My Agent" or "Heyron Setup"
  5. Click "Create"
  6. Important: Copy the API key immediately — it starts with am_
    • This is the only time you'll see the full key
    • Store it somewhere safe (you'll paste it in the next step)
    • If you lose it, just create a new one

Step 3: Give Your Agent the API Key

Tell your agent:

"Connect to AgentMail. My API key is: am_xxxxxxxxxxxx"

Your agent will:

  1. Test the connection to AgentMail
  2. Create its first inbox (like agent-123@agentmail.to)
  3. Send you a test email to confirm everything works
  4. Store the key securely for future use

What just happened? Your agent now has its own email address and can send/receive emails.

Test It

Verification Test

Ask your agent: "Send me a test email using AgentMail"

What should happen:

  • You receive an email from your agent's address
  • The email appears in your regular inbox (Gmail, etc.)
  • Your agent confirms the send was successful

Test Receiving Email

  1. Ask your agent: "What's my AgentMail email address?"
  2. Copy that address (it looks like something@agentmail.to)
  3. From your regular email, send a message to that address
  4. Tell your agent: "Check my AgentMail inbox"

What should happen: Your agent sees your email and can read it back to you or act on it.

Common Issues

Problem Solution
"API key not working" Make sure you copied the entire key starting with am_. If lost, generate a new one in the console.
"Can't create inbox" Check your console — you may have hit the free tier limit. Delete unused inboxes or upgrade.
"Emails not arriving" Check your spam folder. AgentMail emails sometimes land there initially. Mark as "Not spam" to fix.
"Agent says it sent email but I don't see it" Wait 30 seconds — email delivery takes time. Check spam. Ask agent to verify the send was successful.

Security Notes

  • Never share your API key in public chats, Discord, or screenshots
  • If exposed: Delete the key in the AgentMail console immediately and create a new one
  • Your agent's emails: These are real emails — be mindful of what your agent sends
  • Free tier limits: AgentMail has usage limits on the free plan. Check the console for your current usage

What You Can Do Now

  • Receive daily/weekly summaries from your agent
  • Email documents to your agent for analysis
  • Get notifications when your agent completes tasks
  • Have your agent communicate with services that require email
  • Send reminders to yourself through your agent
